Why I Switched to Arch (And Why I'd Do It Again)
Everyone says Arch breaks constantly. They're not wrong. But that's exactly why it's the best learning tool I've found. When your system won't boot after a bad pacman -Syu, you have two choices: panic, or figure out what actually happened. I've learned more about how Linux works from those broken boots than from any course. chroot, mkinitcpio, and the wiki are now muscle memory.

Mar 2026 #networking
Running Wireshark on Your Own Traffic Is Humbling
I set up a packet capture on my home network expecting to see clean, boring traffic. What I found instead: three devices phoning home every 90 seconds, a smart TV sending unencrypted metadata, and my own browser leaking more than I'd like. TCP handshakes look a lot different when it's your own data. Now I run Pi-hole + Unbound and actually audit what leaves my LAN.

Feb 2026 #blueteam
Learning Blue Team: SIEM Logs Are Not Boring Once You Know What to Look For
I spun up a Wazuh instance on my homelab and pointed it at a deliberately vulnerable VM. Within an hour I had 400+ alerts. Most were noise. Learning to tell the difference — a failed SSH login at 3am from an unusual IP vs. my own typos — is the actual skill. Blue teaming is about pattern recognition more than anything else. I'm building that eye slowly, log by log.
